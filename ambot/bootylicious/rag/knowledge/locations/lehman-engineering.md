# Lehman Engineering and Technology Center

The Lehman Engineering and Technology Center is the primary engineering building at ERAU's Daytona Beach campus. It houses the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science on the third floor, along with numerous research and teaching laboratories. The building faces Clyde Morris Boulevard on its east side.

## EECS Department Office
- **also known as**: EECS Office, Department Office, 340 Suite
- **floor**: 3
- **room**: 340

The Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science main office suite. This is where you'll find the department chair, administrative staff, and front desk. Located on the east side of the building facing Clyde Morris Boulevard.

## Robotics and Autonomous Systems Laboratory
- **also known as**: RASL, Robotics Lab
- **floor**: 3

The Robotics and Autonomous Systems Laboratory (RASL) supports research activities into the design and operation of uncrewed and automated systems. This is where the AMBOT project and other robotics research takes place. Students work on autonomous vehicles, drones, and robotic platforms.

## Cybersecurity Engineering Laboratory
- **also known as**: Cyber Lab, Cybersecurity Lab
- **floor**: 3

Furnished with state-of-the-art hardware and software for cybersecurity attack and defend exercises. Students participate in national cybersecurity competitions and conduct research on aviation ecosystem security.

## Digital Systems Laboratory
- **also known as**: Digital Lab, FPGA Lab
- **floor**: 3

Real-time embedded software development and research in digital electronics, microcontrollers and field-programmable gate arrays (FPGAs). Used for courses in computer engineering and embedded systems design.

## EECS Capstone Design Laboratory
- **also known as**: Capstone Lab, Senior Design Lab
- **floor**: 3

Students in the EECS programs work on their multidisciplinary capstone design projects here. Senior design teams build and test final projects spanning hardware, software, and systems integration.

## Software Development Lab
- **also known as**: Software Lab, CS Lab
- **floor**: 3

A multidisciplinary environment for instruction and experiential learning in software development. Used for courses in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, and Aerospace Engineering software projects.

## Circuits and Electronics Laboratory
- **also known as**: Circuits Lab, EE Lab
- **floor**: 3

Introductory courses in electrical engineering and computer engineering use this lab. Students learn circuit analysis, component testing, and basic electronics prototyping.

## Circuits, Sensor and Instrumentation Laboratory
- **also known as**: CSIL, Sensors Lab, Instrumentation Lab
- **floor**: 3

Supports circuit design, fabrication, component testing, and controlled sensor characterization. Used for advanced coursework in instrumentation and sensor systems.

## Radar and Microwaves Laboratory
- **also known as**: Radar Lab, RF Lab, Microwaves Lab
- **floor**: 3

High frequency systems are taught through applied projects as part of the electrical engineering curriculum. Research focuses on radar systems, antenna design, and microwave circuits.

## Speech and Language AI Lab
- **also known as**: SaLAI Lab, NLP Lab
- **floor**: 3

Advancing speech and language AI research at Embry-Riddle. Research focuses on natural language processing, speech recognition, conversational AI systems, and language understanding.

## Flexible and Intelligent Complex Systems Lab
- **also known as**: FICS Lab, Complex Systems Lab
- **floor**: 3

Works on complex systems, autonomy, artificial intelligence, machine learning and their applications to aerospace and engineering problems.

## Special Projects Laboratory
- **also known as**: Special Projects Lab, Student Projects Lab
- **floor**: 3

A working space for student projects and clubs associated with the EECS Department. IEEE, Robotics Association, and other student organizations use this space for competition preparation and project work.

## Engineering Student Projects Center
- **also known as**: ESPC, Student Projects Center, Maker Space
- **floor**: 1

Hands-on exploration and fabrication of engineering designs and solutions. Students come here to build physical prototypes, use 3D printers, laser cutters, and other fabrication tools for their engineering projects.
